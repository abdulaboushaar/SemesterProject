Latest Scams and Updates

Featured Scams

Streaming Service Phishing Emails

Scammers send fake messages that look like Netflix or other streaming services. They claim there is a problem with your account and push you to “update” your payment details on a fake website.

Learn more

Fake Delivery Text Messages

Text messages pretend to be from delivery companies and say you missed a package or need to pay a small fee. The link goes to a fake tracking page that steals your personal and payment information.

Learn more

Passwordless Logins and Passkeys

Large companies are rolling out passkeys so you can log in without passwords. Passkeys are harder to steal and can protect you from many phishing attacks.

Learn more

This Month’s Biggest Hacks

Streaming Service Phishing Scam

Many users reported emails that claimed their streaming account would be closed unless they clicked a link. The link went to a fake site that collected account and payment details.

Learn more

Fake Delivery Notifications

Fake shipping emails and texts are still common. They often say a package is “on hold” and ask you to pay a small fee to release it, but the payment page is controlled by scammers.

Learn more

New Focus on Passkeys

Some companies are promoting passkeys and other passwordless options. These methods link your login to your device and make it harder for attackers to reuse stolen passwords.
