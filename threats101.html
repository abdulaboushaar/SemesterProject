Threats 101

This page introduces the most common online threats in simple language so that anyone can understand them. You do not need to be a security expert to spot warning signs and protect yourself. By learning how these attacks work, you will be more confident when checking your email, logging in to accounts, or downloading files.

Below are a few of the threats that most people see in everyday life. Each section explains what the threat looks like, how attackers try to trick you, and what you can do to respond safely.

Phishing Attacks

Phishing happens when someone sends fake emails or messages that look like they are from a real company or friend. The goal is usually to get you to click a link or open a file that steals your login information or installs malware.

  • Check the sender address carefully before you click.
  • Be careful with messages that feel urgent or threatening.
  • Type the website address yourself instead of using the email link.

Malware & Ransomware

Malware is any software that is built to harm or spy on your device. Ransomware is a type of malware that locks your files and demands payment to unlock them. It often spreads through email attachments or unsafe downloads.

  • Keep your operating system and apps up to date.
  • Use trusted antivirus software and run regular scans.
  • Back up important files so you do not lose everything if something goes wrong.

Social Engineering

Social engineering attacks target people instead of computers. Attackers may pretend to be support staff, coworkers, or even family members. They try to create strong emotions like fear or excitement so that you act quickly without thinking.

  • Slow down and verify who you are talking to.
  • Do not share passwords or security codes with anyone.
  • If something feels wrong, hang up or stop replying and contact the company directly.
