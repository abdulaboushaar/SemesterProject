Safe Practices

This page focuses on simple habits that make your online life much safer. Even small changes, like updating passwords or turning on two-factor authentication, can stop many common attacks. You can start with one or two tips today and slowly build stronger security routines over time.

Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Short or repeated passwords are easy for attackers to guess. A strong password uses a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols, and it is not reused on different websites.

  • Avoid using names, birthdays, or simple patterns.
  • Use at least 12 characters when possible.
  • Consider using a password manager to remember them.

Turn On Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication adds a second step when you log in, such as a code sent to your phone or an app. This makes it much harder for someone to break into your account even if they know your password.

  • Look for 2FA options in the security settings of your accounts.
  • Use an authenticator app instead of SMS when it is available.
  • Keep backup codes in a safe place.

Stay Updated and Use Safe Networks

Updates often fix security bugs that attackers use. Public Wi-Fi can be risky because other people on the network might see your traffic if it is not encrypted.

  • Turn on automatic updates for your phone and computer.
  • Update your browser and extensions regularly.
  • Avoid logging into banking sites on public Wi-Fi, or use a VPN.
